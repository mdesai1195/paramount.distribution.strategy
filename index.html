Executive Summary
The Situation
Paramount sits at a critical inflection point. The Skydance merger has reset the strategic clock, subscriber growth on P+ has slowed, and competitors are aggressively locking in distribution partnerships. The window to establish durable distribution advantages appears to be narrowing as competitors accelerate their own partnership strategies.
The Opportunity
Paramount holds a distinctive asset in its 80M+ Pluto TV users who already consume Paramount IP for free. Few competitors combine a FAST platform of this scale with a fully owned SVOD service, giving Paramount one of the most powerful free-to-paid funnels in the market. Activating it through distribution partnerships could represent one of the highest-leverage growth opportunities available to the business.
The Recommendation
The most compelling opportunity appears to be activating the Pluto to P+ conversion flywheel through carrier bundles, CTV placement deals, and programmatic conversion — paired with a phased push into international markets where Paramount's IP resonates and local competition is less entrenched.

04

White Space Opportunities

01

Telco Bundle Expansion

Verizon is active but T-Mobile and AT&T remain underpenetrated. Wireless carriers need differentiated content to reduce churn. Paramount's CBS sports plus Pluto free tier is a compelling package.

A potential area to explore: expanding carrier bundle conversations to T-Mobile and AT&T, using Pluto as a low-friction free entry point with a P+ upsell pathway built in.
02

Pluto to P+ Conversion Flywheel

80M+ Pluto users are a massive top-of-funnel asset that is being underleveraged. Smart content windowing and in-app upgrade prompts could drive significant low-cost P+ subscriber acquisition.

One approach worth modeling: a programmatic conversion layer where Pluto users who engage heavily with Paramount IP receive contextual P+ trial offers at high-intent content moments.
03

International Distribution Gaps

SkyShowtime covers parts of Europe but APAC and LATAM are significantly underdeveloped. These are high-growth streaming markets where Paramount's IP resonates and local competition is weaker.

LATAM telco bundle deals could be a natural starting point given Nickelodeon and MTV brand recognition in the region; APAC may warrant a JV or licensing model evaluation depending on infrastructure maturity.
04

CTV Platform Placement Deals

Roku and Amazon Fire TV home screen placement drives disproportionate subscriber acquisition. Paramount's negotiating leverage has grown post-Skydance now is the time to renegotiate CTV deals.

Upcoming CTV contract renewals could present an opportunity to revisit revenue share terms and explore whether Pluto TV placement could be structured alongside P+ deals for mutual leverage.
05

The Anti-Netflix Bundle

Peacock, Max, and Paramount+ are the three mid-tier streamers most threatened by Netflix. A formal bundle would create a credible alternative at a competitive price point.

A joint distribution bundle with Peacock and/or Max is worth evaluating as a potential path to reducing churn, improving ARPU, and creating a more credible alternative to Netflix at scale.
06

Live Sports as Distribution Anchor

CBS's NFL rights are Paramount's most powerful distribution lever. Live sports drives both linear and streaming subscriber acquisition and forces distribution partners to carry Paramount products.

NFL and CBS rights may represent a meaningful negotiating asset in distribution deal renewals — worth assessing whether P+ inclusion could be structured into CBS carriage conversations.

The Strategic Bet

◆ Recommended Priority

The most compelling opportunity may be activating the Pluto to Paramount+ flywheel through distribution partnerships — turning 80M free users into a highly efficient paid subscriber acquisition engine.

Paramount appears to hold an underutilized asset in its 80M monthly Pluto TV users who already engage with Paramount IP for free. Few competitors combine a FAST platform at Pluto's scale with a fully owned SVOD service. The opportunity worth exploring is whether building the right distribution infrastructure — carrier bundles, CTV placement deals, and programmatic conversion — could systematically move Pluto users up the value chain into Paramount+ subscriptions.

Key Risks to Watch
Partner Dependency
Over-reliance on a single CTV or telco partner creates leverage risk in future contract renegotiations.
Subscriber Attribution
Bundled subscribers complicate true CAC and LTV measurement, making it harder to assess which distribution channels generate lasting value.
Pluto Brand Dilution
Aggressively pushing Pluto users toward P+ risks undermining Pluto's free, low-friction value proposition and driving churn on both products.
